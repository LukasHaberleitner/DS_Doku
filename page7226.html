How many modes are necessary?

When you create a frequency simulation, you choose the number of modes to calculate. Frequency modes response results taper off exponentially after the first mode, but there are local peaks that correspond to natural frequencies of the model. You should use good engineering judgment to help decide how many modes are appropriate. If you are running a first test or prototype and rough results are OK, a single mode may be enough. If you are uncertain or require more accurate results, more modes are better. However, you should also consider computational costs. Each modal calculation can add significant computation time to the solution.

The expected usage is to have a total effective mass ratio greater than 80%, at least. This use is mainly valid if the excitation is distributed on the whole structure (such as a restraint excitation generating a global acceleration).
