Updating Documents

This task contains information on updating documents.
The connections in your design will not update automatically after you make certain changes to it, such as moving parts, runs or lofts. This behavior is by design, to enhance performance. In order to update your design document click the Force Update button .

This command can also be used to update a run. If you have several contiguous parts placed on a run, then modifying, using copy-paste or deleting a part may cause the connector symbol to disappear from the adjacent part. Updating the run will bring the connector visualization back. NOTE: The symbol only displays when you have an option selected. Go to Tools - Options - Equipment & Systems, select the Display tab, and select the option Part connectors.

You can also update a part - see Placing Parts.
  Table below lists the difference between Update Part functionality and Force Update functionality.
 
  Update Part Force Update
What this functionality is? Update Part is specific to Equipment and System. This functionality will update equipment and system's connections (e.g. fluidic connections, run connections). Force Update functionality will update only mechanical and geometric entities (e.g. constraints, geometrical updates), equipment and system related elements can not be updated using this functionality.
Which element is updated? Update Part updates only runs, bendable, flexible and tubes. Force Update updates all parts under the active product.
What portion is updated? In Update Part connections are updated. In Force Update geometry and mechanical constraints are updated.
  Note: Update Part is for updating the connections in equipment and system and not the parts. As a result, parts such as flexible and bendable may change locations after Update Part.
