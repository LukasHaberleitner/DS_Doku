About the Lock Symmetry

When activated, Lock Symmetry lets you work on a delimited zone, the operation results being automatically duplicated from that work zone to the mirror one.
  This symmetry is determined by the cells coverage of the virtual plies, symbolized by three zones:
  • Upper zone: that is the work zone at the top of the table, colored in yellow.
  • Lower zone: that is the mirror zone, at the bottom of the table, colored in grey.
    Results are duplicated to this zone.
    You cannot make selections in that zone.
  • Neutral zone: the zone in the middle of the table, that contains the rest of the virtual (non-symmetrical) plies.
    You work in that zone as you would in a non-lock symmetry context.
When Lock Symmetry is active:
  • Multiple Row Edition does not work.
  • Recomputation from Grid de-activates Lock Symmetry.
  • Merge domains gives a result if virtual plies (not only virtual sequences) are symmetrical.
