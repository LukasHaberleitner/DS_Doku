Defining the Color of Elements


You can apply a color to specific vertices and edges of a surface to differentiate them from other surface elements.

  1. 1. From the Operations toolbar, click Color Zone Definition .

  2. In the Vertex Color panel, select a color.
      Tip: Click More colors from the list to select others colors or to define customized colors.

  3. Select the faces, vertices, or edges to color.
    From Tools Palette, you can: 
    • Click Face Selection , Edge Selection , or Vertex Selection  to select the type of element to color.
    • Click Automatic Selection to select any type of surface element.
    • Click Linear Propagation , Loop Propagation , or Fill Propagation to select several elements at once.
      Notes: 
     
    • You cannot select Fill Propagation if you selected Edge Selection.
    • In the tree, a Color Zone element appears under the subdivision surface after selection of any element.
      If you select faces, the color appears only on the edges and vertices of the faces.

  4.  To swap the selection of elements, click Selection Inversion .

  5. To activate or deactivate the modification of the elements included in a working zone, click Working Zone Activation / Deactivation.

  6. To reset the color of the selected elements, click Color Zone Removal .

  7. Click Apply to validate the definition of the color zone.
  8. Define other color zones.
  9. Press Escape.
  Note: If you modify the subdivision surface, edit the existing color zones or create new ones to color the new surface elements.
