Realistic Shape Optimizer in a Nutshell

The main application fields of Realistic Shape Optimizer are:

  • Ability to represent parts under load conditions,

  • Ability to perform a reverse analysis to optimize tooling (Die, Cast, Mold, ...),

  • Ability to optimize the part definition, based on "as built" measurements compared to CAD initial definition.

Be aware that the morphing does not preserve the flatness of a surface.

Moreover, the deformation may not be linear between two vectors. In empty spaces where vectors do not define a deformation, the deformation is interpolated from the nearest vectors. This may result in the deformation of flat areas.

In this example:
  • The initial surface is in blue.
  • The field of vectors in green.
  • The interpolation vectors in yellow.
  • The result morphing in orange.

To ensure that the morphing has the expected result, make sure the vectors field contains enough vectors to describe the deformation.

Realistic Shape Optimizer is not meant for large deformations. The app provides the best results with small deformations.

Large distortions in the vectors field you use can lead to bad quality results.
Very large distortions in the vector field can result in geometry deformed in a negative X-direction, despite all the vectors having positive coordinates.

For an optimum quality of the deformed geometry, make sure that

  • The values in the deviation vectors fields are relatively small compared to the model dimensions.
  • The deviation vectors field represents a physical deformation.
  • The deviation vectors field has only a small distortion.

To improve the resulting geometry:

  • Remove a percentage of vectors.
  • Apply a global smoothing.
  • Create and apply a filter with criteria such as length or distortions to remove vectors.
