Defining Manipulation Laws

This task shows you how to define and apply a manipulation law to a surface and propagate the deformation to the surrounding elements.

  • Create a 3D shape containing a subdivision surface.

  • Define the number of cuts for your subdivision surface.

  •  From the Modification toolbar, click Modification .

  • In the Tools Palette, click Law Manipulation On/Off .

    This function is available with all types of the Translation, Rotation, and Affinity commands.

    The law manipulator lets you define and manage the manipulation laws. You can define your own laws or choose predefined laws such as linear, concave, or convex laws.

    Symbol Description
    1 Moves the graphic manipulator in the work area.
    2 Locks free edges.
    3 Locks sharp edges.
    4 Shows predefined laws.
    5 Defines the propagation method and coefficients of the law:
    Symbol Description
    propagates to the free edges around the selection.
    propagates to one or more rings of edges around the selection.
    propagates to all the elements within a spherical area around the selection.

    Notes:
    • A circle representing the outer contour of the sphere appears around any selected vertex.
    • You can move the handle along the axis to modify the sphere radius.
    • You can also edit the sphere radius using manipulation law input box.
    6 Removes a coefficient.
    7 Opens a dialog box to enter specific coefficients. If you select the third selection, you can also enter the sphere radius.
    8 Saves a law.
    9 Stores all the saved laws and lets you either choose one of them using the left and right arrows, or delete one.
    10 Adds a coefficient.

  • Define a propagation law.
    • You can change the size of the law manipulator with the resizing handle at the top.
    • You can also modify the coefficients manually by manipulating the white handles.
    • For better visualization, the impacted vertices are displayed in colors.
    • The laws you create are automatically named and ordered by number.
    • The number of coefficient is limited to 10.

  • After you define a law, select a face, an edge, or a vertex and drag the arrow in the required direction.
    You can also select multiple elements.

  • Without a law, only the selected elements move.

  • With a law, the elements surrounding your selection move according to the coefficients defined by the law.
