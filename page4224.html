Breaking or Deleting Groups

This procedure describes how to break a group of activities within an inspect process.

Breaking a group means to disassociate the activities within the group from the group and to eliminate the logical entity that is the specific group.  The activities within the group are not deleted, however.  To delete both the group and all the activities within it, see Deleting a Group.

A group can consist of either activities or groups.  Breaking a group that contains subgroups does not affect the sub-groupings.
This procedure assumes that you have created a group.

  1. Select the group you want to break.

  2. Using the right mouse button, select Group - group_name object > Break Group.

    The Group activity disappears from the PPR tree, although the activities or subgroups within the group remain visible.

 
 

Deleting a Group
  Deleting a group deletes both the group and all the activities within it.  To retain the activities but to eliminate their grouping, see Breaking a Group.
 

  1. Select the group in the PPR tree.

  2. Press Delete.

    3. You can also use the right mouse button and select Delete.
      The group and all the activities in it are deleted.  If you select a group that contains groups, all the subgroups and activities are deleted.
