Creating Geometric Elements from Surface Elements

You can also associate the geometric elements to the surface elements to modify the subdivision surface accordingly.
This task shows you how to:
  • Create Lines from Surface Elements
  • Create Circles from Surface Elements
  • Create Curves from Surface Elements
  • Create Planes from Surface Elements
Create a 3D shape containing a subdivision surface.
 

Create Lines from Surface Elements

  1. From the Operations toolbar, click Geometry from Mesh .

  2. Select a subdivision surface.

  3. Click Line from Mesh .

  4. Select points on the subdivision surface. To do so:
    • Select at least one surface.
    • Select at least two edges.
    • Select at least two vertices.

    From the Tools Palette, you can:
    • Click Face Selection , Edge Selection , or Vertex Selection to select a specific type of surface element.
    • Click Linear Propagation , Loop Propagation , or Fill Propagation to select several elements at once. For more information, see Propagation.
    Note: You cannot select Fill Propagation if you selected Edge Selection.

    The line appears in the 3D area.

  5. Optional: Click Link to associate the line to the selected surface elements.

    If you move the line, the subdivision surface moves accordingly.

  6. Click Apply to validate the creation of the geometric element.

    7. The line appears in the tree.
 

Create Circles from Surface Elements

  1. From the Operations toolbar, click Geometry from Mesh .

  2. Select a subdivision surface.

  3. Click Circle from Mesh .

  4. Select at least three points on the subdivision surface. To do so:
    • Select at least one surface.
    • Select at least two edges.
    • Select at least three adjacent vertices.

    From the Tools Palette, you can:
    • Click Face Selection , Edge Selection , or Vertex Selection to select a specific type of surface element.
    • Click Linear Propagation , Loop Propagation , or Fill Propagation to select several elements at once. For more information, see Propagation.
    Note: You cannot select Fill Propagation if you selected Edge Selection.

    The circle appears in the 3D area.
    The plane of the circle corresponds to the median plane of the set of points.

  5. Optional: Click Link to associate the line to the selected surface elements.

    If you move the circle, the subdivision surface moves accordingly. 

  6. Click Apply to validate the creation of the geometric element.

    7. The circle appears in the tree.
 

Create Curves from Surface Elements

  1. From the Operations toolbar, click Geometry from Mesh .

  2. Select a subdivision surface.

  3. Click Curve from Mesh .

  4. Select at least three adjacent points on the subdivision surface. To do so:
    • Select at least two adjacent edges.
    • Select at least three adjacent vertices.

    From the Tools Palette, you can:
    • Click Edge Selection , or Vertex Selection to select a specific type of surface element.
    • Click Linear Propagation , Loop Propagation , or Fill Propagation to select several elements at once. For more information, see Propagation.
    Note: You cannot select Fill Propagation if you selected Edge Selection.

    The curve appears in the 3D area.

  5. Optional: Click Planarity to create a plane that includes the points of the curve.

    The curve plane appears in the 3D area.

    The curve points are projected onto the median plane, and linked geometrically according to a minimum distance between the points.

  6. Optional: Click Link to associate the line to the selected surface elements.

    If you move the curve, the subdivision surface moves accordingly. 

  7. Click Apply to validate the creation of the geometric element.

    8. The styling curve appears in the tree.
 

Create Planes from Surface Elements

  1. From the Operations toolbar, click Geometry from Mesh .

  2. Select a subdivision surface.

  3. Click Plane from Mesh .

  4. Select points on the subdivision surface. To do so:
    • Select at least one surface.
    • Select at least two edges.
    • Select at least three vertices.

    From the Tools Palette, you can:
    • Click Face Selection , Edge Selection , or Vertex Selection to select a specific type of surface element.
    • Click Linear Propagation , Loop Propagation , or Fill Propagation to select several elements at once. For more information, see Propagation.
    Note: You cannot select Fill Propagation if you selected Edge Selection.

    The plane appears in the 3D area.
    The plane corresponds to the median plane of the set of points.

  5. Optional: Click Axis Permutation to change the orientation of the plane by rotating the directions of the x, y, and z-axes of the Robot.

    The orientation of the plane updates automatically in the 3D area.

  6. Optional: Click Link to associate the plane to the selected surface elements.

    If you move the plane, the subdivision surface moves accordingly.

  7. Click Apply to validate the creation of the geometric element.

    8. The plane appears in the tree.
