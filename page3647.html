Creating a Transition Specification

You can create single integral feature connecting two or more adjacent integral surfaces.
   
  1. Click the Transition Specification from the Symbols toolbar.

     

  2. Select one or two elements defining the edge, to determine the position of the leader's anchor point. 

  3. Move the pointer and position the symbol, or snap the leader orientation on one of the privileged directions and position the symbol.
     
    • If the leader is associated with two line references, it can be snapped on the horizontal and vertical directions, and also on the angle bisector line direction between the two line references. In this case, a positional link is created between the leader and the two line references.

  4. In the Transition Specification dialog box, specify the values in the required zone boxes.


    Field Numbers Significance
    1 and 6 Centre indication area lets you apply the transition feature.
    lets you specify a single indication for all the edges of an object.
    2 and 7 Upper main indication area lets you apply the transition feature to the upper adjacent feature 9.
    3 and 8 Lower main indication area lets you apply the transition feature to the lower adjacent feature 10.
    4 Upper primary reference section indication area. The indications in the upper areas refer to the adjacent feature in face view.
    5 Lower primary reference section indication area. The indications in the lower areas refer to the other adjacent feature.
    9 Upper adjecent feature.
    10 Lower adjecent feature.

    The values entered for the zones are saved for the next usage.

  5. Right-click the transition specification symbol and select Properties.
  6. From the Anchor Point list, select the position of the anchor point to specify the position of the annotation.
The graphic properties of such symbols can be specified. For more information about text properties, see Text Properties.
